## What this is, and how it relates to competitor-analysis

A one-versus-one, sales-ready card for a specific competitor or incumbent, used in a live bid or pitch. `competitor-analysis` maps the whole landscape; this is the focused tool for a single matchup. Tie every claim to something the customer selects on, so the card reinforces the value proposition rather than listing features.

## Method

1. **Research the competitor** (web search plus any provided win/loss notes): offering, commercial model, positioning, recent moves, strengths and weaknesses, the route they hold to market.
2. **Build the card:**
   - **Overview:** who they are, their target, their positioning in one line.
   - **Quick comparison:** capability by capability, us, them, who wins.
   - **Where we win:** each advantage with a proof point, against what the customer selects on.
   - **Where they win:** their strengths with our honest counter.
   - **Objections and responses:** what the customer says, how we answer (value, total cost of ownership, risk).
   - **Questions to ask:** prompts that surface where we are strong and they are weak.
   - **Win/loss patterns:** when we win, when we lose, what tips a competitive decision.
3. **Keep it scannable:** tables, short bullets, usable mid-conversation.

## Notes

- **Confidence reads as risk.** Buyers interpret certainty through a risk lens; over-claiming maturity kills credibility. Pitch and objection-handling should use bounded confidence with the uncertainty acknowledged, which reduces perceived risk more than optimism does.
- Pairs with `competitor-analysis`, `value-proposition`, `strategy-red-team`.

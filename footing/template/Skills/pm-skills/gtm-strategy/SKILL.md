## What this is

A go-to-market plan, sector-neutral but built for a business selling into other organisations, not just direct-to-consumer.

## For contract and tender-driven sales

- **Route to contract, not just consumer channels.** Where the business sells through tenders, frameworks, procurement panels or long B2B sales cycles, the "channels" that matter are those routes, the incumbents you compete against or partner with, direct-to-customer relationships, and the people who carry the decision to contract. Paid search and viral loops are rarely the point in this context.
- **A campaign against a live opportunity, not a launch event.** For programme or contract-based sales, a "launch" is usually a targeted campaign against a specific opportunity: positioning, references, trials or pilots, and the bid itself, run over a long cycle rather than a single go-live.
- **Message-market fit.** Messaging leads with what the offering delivers against what the buying committee actually selects on, and respects any confidentiality or disclosure constraints the client's sector imposes.

## Method

1. **Gather research:** the offering, the target segment, validation data, the competitive and incumbent picture.
2. **Choose the route to market:** the tenders, frameworks, direct routes and partnerships that reach the customer; focus on a few done well.
3. **Develop messaging:** the core value proposition for the segment (use `value-proposition`), the differentiators, the proof points, by channel.
4. **Define success metrics:** awareness, engagement, qualified opportunities, and the contract metrics that actually matter; set a baseline first.
5. **Build a phased plan:** pre-launch, launch or first campaign, post-launch momentum, with go/no-go decision points; a 90-day roadmap.

Render the plan.

## Two qualifying tests before you invest

- **Interest is not budget authority.** Before committing effort to a route, qualify whether it is actually funded, at what order of magnitude, and on what timeframe. A funded route to contract is the test, not a warm meeting or polite interest.
- **Chase sponsorship, not visibility.** Being known (visibility) or supported (advocacy) does not move a deal forward. Progress comes when someone inside the buying organisation carries risk and ownership on your behalf (sponsorship). Plan the engagement to convert visibility into sponsorship, and map it with `stakeholder-map`.

- Start with the most confident segment; validate before scaling.

## What this is, and is not

This is the **customer-side** segmentation (who hires the offering, for what). For segmenting an existing **user** base by behaviour, use `user-segmentation`; the two are companions and should produce compatible segments.

## The framing to use

The upstream skill lists behavioural, demographic and firmographic dimensions side by side. Lead with **purpose**. Produce a small number of segments named for the job customers hire the offering for, each of which would select on a recognisably different set of criteria. If two proposed segments select on the same things, they are one segment. Firmographics and procurement route are recorded as attributes of a segment, not as the segmentation itself.

## Method

1. **Explore the addressable market** for the offering: the full set of purposes it could serve.
2. **Choose the purpose dimensions** that actually divide customers by what they select on.
3. **Define 3 to 5 distinct, non-overlapping segments**, each purpose-named.
4. **Characterise each** and validate distinctness.
5. **Assess the opportunity** per segment: size, trajectory, competitive intensity.

## For each segment

- **Name and overview:** a clear, purpose-led identifier; a size estimate where data allows; the trajectory.
- **Customer and user:** who selects and who operates in this segment.
- **Purpose (job to be done):** the primary job, its context, frequency and stakes.
- **Selection criteria:** what this segment selects on, with thresholds where you can get them.
- **Pains and obstacles:** barriers to the job, and the cost of not solving them.
- **Fit:** how well the offering serves this segment, and the adoption barriers.
- **Competitive picture:** the incumbents, or workarounds in play (hands off to `competitor-analysis`).

## Notes

- Segments must be distinct, reachable, and selectable-on; drop any that are not.
- A segmentation from thin evidence is a hypothesis; say so.
- Pairs with `user-segmentation`, `user-personas`, `ideal-customer-profile`, `beachhead-segment`, `market-sizing`.

# Estimate Market Size (TAM, SAM, SOM)

## What this is

You size the opportunity, for an investment case, a business case, or a market-entry decision.

## Sizing for a contract or programme-based sale

The TAM, SAM, SOM method holds regardless of sector, but where the business sells into larger organisations through contracts, tenders or framework agreements rather than open-market retail, size against **budgets and programmes**, not a generic total-addressable-market figure:

- **TAM**, the total relevant spend: the category, programme or budget line the offering competes for, read from published sector spend, market reports and category data.
- **SAM**, the portion realistically serviceable, gated by accreditation, certification, framework or panel access where the sector requires it, and by product or service capability.
- **SOM**, the portion obtainable in one to three years, gated by incumbents, the route to contract, and the gap between a promising pilot and being awarded ongoing budget, a pattern common to any programme or contract-based sale.

## Method

1. **Define the market:** the problem space, the segment, the geography and the constraints.
2. **Top-down:** start from the total programme or category spend and narrow to the relevant slice. Cite sources.
3. **Bottom-up:** build from units (customers by contract value by frequency) to cross-validate.
4. **Scope SAM:** the slice the offering can serve given accreditation, framework access and capability.
5. **Estimate SOM:** the achievable share given the competitive and procurement reality.
6. **Project growth:** how each may move over two to three years.
7. **Surface assumptions:** number them, rate confidence, and say how to validate the most uncertain.

## Output

A market definition; TAM, SAM and SOM each with method, sources and reasoning; a summary table (current and 2 to 3 year projection); the growth drivers; and the numbered key assumptions with confidence levels and validation steps.

## Notes

- Always give both top-down and bottom-up; triangulate.
- Use web research for current market and category data; cite sources, never assert unsupported numbers.
- Label estimates against data, and flag wide confidence intervals.
- An opportunity can have a large TAM and a tiny SOM because of the gap between pilot and scaled contract; say so plainly.
- Ground the budget picture in current published sector data and market reports.

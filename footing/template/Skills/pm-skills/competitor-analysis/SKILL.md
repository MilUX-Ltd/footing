## What this is

You map the competitive picture, usually as part of segmentation, a sales process, or a strategy review.

## The framing to use

The competitive set is incumbents, in-house build, and do-nothing, as much as named rivals. Read the route to purchase: who holds the existing relationship, who the budget holder defaults to, who an established player would refer the business to. Tie every differentiation opportunity back to something the segment actually selects on, so the analysis feeds the value proposition rather than producing a generic feature grid.

## Method

1. **Scope the market** for the offering and the segment in question.
2. **Identify the competitive set:** around five direct competitors, plus the incumbents and workarounds that actually compete for the business. Use web research.
3. **Profile each:** positioning, capabilities, pricing or commercial model, route to market, track record.
4. **Strengths and weaknesses:** what each does well, where it falls short against what the segment selects on, where customers are dissatisfied.
5. **Differentiation mapping:** the unmet needs and the criteria no one is meeting well; where you can stand out.
6. **Synthesis:** the competitive dynamics, the threats to watch, the 12 to 18 month picture.

## Output

A market overview; the competitive set with positions; a profile per competitor (capabilities, weaknesses, commercial model, threat); the differentiation opportunities tied to what the customer selects on; and a positioning recommendation with the criteria to emphasise and the threats to monitor.

## Notes

- Distinguish direct competitors from incumbents and the status quo; all compete for the decision.
- Validate across multiple sources; cite them.
- Pairs with `market-segments`, `value-proposition`, `strategy-red-team`.

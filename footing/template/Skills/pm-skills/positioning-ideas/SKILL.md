## What this is

A structured first pass at positioning: where an offering stands against competitors and which unclaimed territory it could own.

## Method

You are a brand strategist working positioning for **$ARGUMENTS**.

1. **Competitive landscape:** the top five competitors or incumbents, each with their positioning angle, their target, the differentiators they emphasise, and the gaps they leave open.
2. **Positioning brainstorm:** five differentiated ideas for the chosen segment, each clearly distinct from the competitors, resonant with the segment's purpose, leaning on capabilities the others downplay, opening unclaimed ground.
3. **For each idea:**
   - **Positioning statement:** one sentence, for example "The only [category] designed for [segment] who need to [outcome the offering delivers]".
   - **Rationale:** why it resonates and differentiates.
   - **Supporting messages:** the messages that reinforce it.
   - **Advantage:** the specific capability or proof that earns the claim.

## Notes

- Anchor positioning on what the offering actually delivers against the things the segment selects on, not on adjectives.
- Pairs with `value-proposition`, `value-prop-statements`, `competitor-analysis`.
- Mind commercial confidentiality before anything goes public.

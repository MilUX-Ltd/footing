## What this is, and how it relates to value-proposition

`value-proposition` works out *what* the value is, one segment at a time, as how well the offering fits what the customer selects on. This skill turns that into the *copy*: statements for marketing, sales and onboarding. Run them in that order.

## Method

From the value proposition(s) for **$ARGUMENTS**, write statements that each:

- Address one specific segment or use case.
- Lead with the primary benefit and the outcome delivered.
- Name the capability that makes it possible.
- Use clear, compelling language in the audience's terms.

Produce a statement per segment and per context (marketing, sales, onboarding). Keep the customer and the user distinct; the statement that persuades a budget holder is not the one that reassures a day-to-day user.

## Worked example

- **For a budget holder:** "[Offering] gives your team a shared view of the numbers that holds up without manual reconciliation, works with your existing systems, so month-end runs off one shared picture instead of spreadsheets and emails, at a fraction of the ongoing cost of the incumbent alternative."
- **For a day-to-day user:** "[Offering] works the way you already do the job: quick to pick up, low training burden, one click to share what you're looking at."

## Notes

- Mind confidentiality before anything goes public: check nothing commercially sensitive is in the copy.

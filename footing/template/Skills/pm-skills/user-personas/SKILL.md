## What this is

You build personas as discovery or design work, grounded in purpose so they slot into segmentation and value-proposition work without translation.

## Two persona types

The upstream skill builds three generic user personas. For most B2B work, it is more useful to build personas in two types and be explicit about which is which, since the person who buys is often not the person who uses:

- **Customer persona**, the person who selects and pays: a procurement lead, budget holder, department head, or business owner. Their purpose is to acquire something that meets a need within their constraints. They judge on selection criteria: total cost, ease of procurement, risk, supplier track record.
- **User persona**, the person who operates the thing day to day: an employee, a specialist, a frontline worker, a member of the public the offering serves. Their purpose is to do the task the offering supports. They judge on the experience of using it under real working conditions.

Map at least one of each where both matter. Inside a segment you may have one customer persona and one or two user personas.

## Method

1. **Read the evidence.** Interview transcripts, survey responses, support or feedback data, win/loss notes. Extract recurring purposes, pains, gains and behaviours. Ground every insight in data; quote where you can; flag gaps.

2. **Cluster by purpose, not demographics.** Group people by the job they are hiring the service for and the criteria they select on, not by age, seniority or organisation size. Firmographics are recorded as attributes, not as the basis of the persona.

3. **For each persona, capture:**
   - **Type and identity:** customer or user; role, context, and the constraints they work under (budget authority, procurement rules for the customer; day-to-day working conditions for the user).
   - **Primary purpose (job to be done):** the core outcome they are trying to achieve, with its context and frequency.
   - **Top pains:** the obstacles to completing the job, with their impact.
   - **Top gains:** the outcomes they seek, and how they would measure success.
   - **What they judge on:** what this persona actually selects or judges on, with a threshold where you can get one.
   - **One unexpected insight:** a counterintuitive pattern from the data, and why it matters.

4. **Validate.** Cross-reference so personas are grounded, distinct, and non-overlapping. If two personas share the same purpose and criteria, they are one.

## Notes

- Keep customer and user personas visibly separate; the whole point is that they judge the offering differently.
- A persona built on thin evidence is a hypothesis; say so on the artefact.
- Pairs with `market-segments` / `user-segmentation` (the segment) and `customer-journey-map` (the journey the persona travels).

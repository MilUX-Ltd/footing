## What this is

A power/interest map and an engagement plan, for an engagement, a bid, or campaign-audience planning.

## Map the real buying committee, not a generic org chart

Most B2B sales and internal change efforts involve more than one decision-maker. Map the actual cast: the **economic buyer** who holds the budget and signs off, the **user** who operates the thing day to day, the **technical evaluator** who assesses fit, the **champion** who advocates internally, any **blockers**, the **incumbents** you compete against or partner with, and anyone with formal approval authority. Keep the customer (who selects and pays) and the user (who operates) distinct, because they hold different power and different interest.

## Method

1. **Identify stakeholders:** individuals and groups inside and around the customer, plus incumbents, partners, approving authorities and end users.
2. **Classify each** on Power (ability to influence the decision or resources) and Interest (how much it affects them or how engaged they are).
3. **Place on the grid:**

   | | High interest | Low interest |
   |---|---|---|
   | **High power** | Manage closely: involve early, seek input | Keep satisfied: periodic updates, escalate only the critical |
   | **Low power** | Keep informed: regular updates, demos, feedback | Monitor: light touch, available on request |

4. **Per quadrant,** recommend frequency, format, key messages, and the risk if neglected.
5. **Engagement plan table:** stakeholder, role, power, interest, strategy, frequency, channel, key message.
6. **Flag conflicts:** stakeholders with competing interests, and how to align them.

Save as markdown.

## Notes

- **Map each stakeholder to visibility, advocacy or sponsorship.** Being known (visibility) or supported (advocacy) does not move a decision forward; progress comes when someone carries risk and ownership on your behalf (sponsorship). Note which each stakeholder actually offers, and plan to convert toward sponsorship.
- Pairs with `pre-mortem`.

# Pitch Prep

Build a short pitch that starts a conversation, on the three-act structure. The job is to help the founder think, not to write the pitch for them. Ask the question that moves their thinking, then get out of the way. Keep the end user in the room throughout.

The structure this skill runs on is in `Resources/Frameworks/Pitch — the three-act structure.md`. Read it first.

## Step 1 — Frame the pitch

Establish, in one or two quick questions:

- **Audience.** Investors, a demo-day jury, a customer, a partner. The ask changes with the room.
- **Time and format.** A 60-second version, a three-minute pitch, or a longer deck. Default to three minutes.
- **What is being pitched.** The product or idea in one sentence. If they cannot say it in a sentence, that is the first piece of work.

## Step 2 — Draft the 60-second version first

Get the spine down before the full pitch. Five lines:

1. Intro — who are you?
2. Hook — why is this a problem or an opportunity?
3. Solution — what are you doing about it?
4. Value — how do you quantify the value to the customer?
5. Call to action — when do we meet again?

If they cannot fill these five cleanly, the three-minute version will not hold. Fix the spine first.

## Step 3 — Expand to the three acts, on the clock

- **Act I, Set-up (0:00 to 1:00).** Intro and mission, the problem sized, the solution in a line, why now.
- **Act II, Confrontation (1:00 to 2:00).** Product shown working, value quantified, market, honest competition.
- **Act III, Resolution (2:00 to 3:00).** Business model, team, traction, the ask, the one-line vision.

About 150 words a minute, so a three-minute pitch is roughly 450 words. Hold them to the clock.

## Step 4 — Stress-test

Run the idea against the five failure modes (in the framework doc). For each, ask the founder the matching question rather than giving the answer:

- No user in the room. Boiling the ocean. The demo is a diagram. No proof. The soft ask.

The pm-skills in `Skills/pm-skills/` are useful here: value proposition, lean canvas, identify-assumptions, beachhead segment, positioning. Use them to sharpen a beat, not to replace the founder's judgement.

## Step 5 — Build the deck

- The structure comes first; the aesthetic second. Slides are visual aids, not the script.
- Two worked examples to adapt are in `Resources/Templates/`: a dark and a light deck, both following this arc.
- For a distinctive look, browse design-system references at https://getdesign.md/ and build the deck in that style with an HTML-slide tool. One aesthetic per deck.

## Step 6 — Rehearse

Out loud, on a timer, before anything else. Cut to the clock act by act. The ask and the closing line survive; everything else is negotiable.

## What this skill does not do

- It does not write the pitch for the founder. It structures and challenges.
- It does not design the deck. It points to the structure, the examples, and the tools.

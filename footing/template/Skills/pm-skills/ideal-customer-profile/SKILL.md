# Ideal Customer Profile

## What this is

You define the ICP, built on the same segmentation as your personas, journey maps and value propositions, so they all line up.

## The key move

The upstream skill leads with firmographics (company size, revenue, vertical). Invert that. The ICP is the **purpose-segment the offering best fits**: the customers hiring the offering for a job it does well, who select on criteria the offering can meet, and who therefore retain and expand. Firmographics, procurement gates and route to purchase are recorded because they govern whether and how you can sell, but they describe the segment, they do not define it.

## Method

1. **Gather evidence.** Existing customers and wins, interview and discovery notes, win/loss analysis, feedback and churn. Use web research where it helps.

2. **Segment by value, then read the pattern.** Identify the customers who got the most value, retained, expanded, and referred. Read what they have in common: the purpose they hired the offering for, the criteria they selected on, and the conditions that made it land.

3. **Write the ICP:**
   - **Purpose and fit:** the job this customer hires the offering for, and what the offering does well for them. This is the core; everything else supports it.
   - **Firmographic profile:** organisation type, size, industry, procurement route, budget owner. Recorded as the conditions of sale.
   - **Customer and user:** who selects and who operates within this profile, and what each judges on (see `user-personas`).
   - **Buying behaviour:** how they discover, evaluate and decide; the stakeholders; the timeline; the obstacles in the buying process.
   - **Disqualification criteria:** who is explicitly *not* a fit, and why. A real ICP excludes.
   - **Ideal-of-the-ideal:** the highest-value slice within the ICP to concentrate on first (hands off to `beachhead-segment`).

4. **Make it usable.** State the go-to-market implication: where to find these customers, what to lead with, and what to qualify out.

## Notes

- The ICP drives focus; it does not pretend no one else exists. Keep an "acceptable secondary" note where useful.
- Revisit as evidence accumulates; an ICP from three wins is a starting hypothesis.
- Pairs with `market-segments`, `beachhead-segment`, `value-proposition`.

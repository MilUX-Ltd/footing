## What this is, and how it relates to pricing-strategy

This skill explores *which* revenue model to use; `pricing-strategy` sets the *price* once the model is chosen. Use this first to generate and compare models, then hand to `pricing-strategy` for the structure.

## Method

Brainstorm three to five distinct models. For each: how it works for this offering; audience fit and willingness to pay; unit economics (acquisition cost, lifetime value, break-even, margin); risks; competitive position; and a low-cost validation experiment with a success metric. Then prioritise by strategic fit, ease, and validation potential, and recommend one or two to test first.

## Models, including contract-based ones

Freemium, subscription, usage-based, per-seat or per-user, one-time purchase, marketplace or transaction fee, and advertising are the standard set. For services and contract-based delivery, weigh:

- **Day-rate and contracted** delivery, priced inside a client contract or framework rate card.
- **Managed service** with a recurring support fee, which fits ongoing support and staff turnover on the client side.
- **Licensing** of a capability or component to a larger partner, with the partner carrying the route to contract.

Most offerings end up hybrid (for example a contracted day-rate now and a managed-service tail later). Test early; a model is cheaper to change than a customer relationship is to rebuild.

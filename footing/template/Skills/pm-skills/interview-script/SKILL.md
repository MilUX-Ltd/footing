## What this is, and is not

For preparing and running discovery interviews with customers and users. The transcripts feed `summarize-interview`, the personas and the journey map. It is research interviewing, not a meeting debrief.

## The framing to use

- **Interview the customer and the user separately.** They hire the thing for different purposes and judge it on different criteria. A script for a budget holder is not a script for a day-to-day user.
- **Confidentiality aware.** Ask about behaviour and experience, not commercially sensitive detail.
- **Purpose, not opinion.** Mom Test discipline keeps you after the job they hire the thing for and how they judge it, grounded in what they actually did.

## Method

1. **Clarify objectives:** the questions the team needs answered, the decision it informs, the assumptions to test.
2. **Build the script** in sections:
   - **Opening (2 to 3 min):** purpose is learning not selling; no right answers; permission to record; time check.
   - **Warm-up (5 min):** their role, context, a typical week. Build rapport.
   - **Core, jobs to be done (15 to 20 min):** the last time they did the thing, step by step; the tools and people involved; the hardest part; what they have tried; what "good" looks like; what they currently spend in time or money; what they would give up to have it solved.
   - **Probing:** "tell me more", a gentle "why" two or three times, "a specific example", "what happened next", "how did that feel".
   - **Mom Test rules:** ask about their life not your idea; the past not the future; listen 80, talk 20; never pitch; chase strong emotion; treat compliments as noise.
   - **Wrap-up (3 to 5 min):** anything missed; who else to talk to; thanks; next steps.
3. **Customise** to the product area, the persona, and the time available.
4. **Add the note-taking template:** participant, date, key jobs, current solution, biggest pain, desired outcome, willingness to pay or invest, surprise finding, follow-up.

Save the script and the template as markdown.

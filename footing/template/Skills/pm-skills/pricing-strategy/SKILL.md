## What this is

A pricing skill grounded in value delivered, competitive position and willingness to pay.

## Beyond the standard SaaS models

The subscription and per-seat models below still apply, but if you sell through contracts, tenders or long sales cycles rather than self-serve signup, the picture changes:

- **Day rates and contracted rates.** Much services and project-based delivery is priced as day rates or fixed fees inside a wider contract or framework agreement, with rate cards and ceilings set by the client or the framework rather than by you alone. Price against those realities, not a consumer subscription model.
- **Value-based pricing.** Anchor value-based pricing on what the offering delivers (outcome achieved, risk reduced, cost avoided over the life of the relationship), the things the client actually selects on.
- **Procurement constraints.** Procurement rules, budget-approval cycles, and the route to contract bound what is possible. A clever pricing model that cannot be bought through the client's actual purchasing process is not a pricing model.

## Method

1. **Understand the value:** the value proposition, the customer's alternative and its cost, the quantifiable outcome, the willingness to pay it implies.
2. **Choose a model:** flat-rate, per-seat, usage-based, tiered, freemium, freemium-plus-usage, value-based, or day-rate/contracted. Recommend the best fit and why.
3. **Read the competition:** competitor and incumbent pricing, where the offering sits, the gaps and conventions.
4. **Design the structure:** two to four tiers with clear differentiation, the value metric you charge on, anchor pricing, and any annual discount.
5. **Estimate sensitivity:** Van Westendorp where survey data exists, otherwise reason from competitor pricing and value.
6. **Plan experiments:** founder-led sales conversations to test willingness to pay, page tests, conversion analysis by price point.
7. **Output a recommendation:** model, value metric, a tier table (price, segment, features, positioning), the key assumptions and how to test them, and the risks with mitigations.

Save as markdown; flag assumptions that need validating before launch.

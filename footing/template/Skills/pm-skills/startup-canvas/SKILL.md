## What this is, and why it is the default for new ventures

The Startup Canvas separates strategy from business model: the nine strategy sections of the Product Strategy Canvas, plus cost structure and revenue streams. It is the recommended single artefact when a founder needs both strategic clarity and a workable business model, which is most of the time. Use `lean-canvas` when speed alone is wanted, `business-model` for an established business or investor pack, and `product-strategy` when only the strategy is in scope.

## The canvas

Part 1, strategy (nine sections): vision; market segments (purpose-led, customer and user); relative costs; value proposition (what-before, how, what-after, alternatives, expressed as how well it fits the customer's needs); trade-offs; key metrics (the criteria that matter, north-star, the one metric that matters this quarter); growth (and the route to market); capabilities; defensibility (can the integrated strategy be copied).

Part 2, business model: cost structure (recurring and scaling costs); revenue streams (pricing approach, scalability, the biggest uncertainties).

## Method

Work the nine strategy sections, then the two business-model sections, then check coherence: do all the choices reinforce each other, and does the strategy pass the can't/won't-copy test? Surface the must-be-true hypotheses and start testing the riskiest. Render the canvas for the founder to react to.

## Notes

- Keep strategy and business model distinct but connected; that separation is the point of this canvas.

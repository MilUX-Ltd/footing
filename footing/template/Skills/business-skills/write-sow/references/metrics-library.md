# Metrics and acceptance-criteria library

Patterns for measurable acceptance criteria and KPIs, grouped by common service types. Use these as starting points and edit them to your own offer; they are examples, not a fixed taxonomy. The aim every time: a criterion a reviewer can verify without debate.

Every criterion should fix three things: the artefact (what is handed over), the measure (how completion is judged), and the target (the threshold that counts as met).

## Research and discovery

- Findings: research delivered against the N questions agreed in scope, with each finding traceable to its evidence.
- Coverage: at least N interviews or N data sources, sampled across the agreed segments; a stated method.
- Output: a synthesis (report, map, or model) at the agreed fidelity, in the agreed format, with prioritised recommendations.

## Design

- Research-to-design traceability: every design decision maps to a stated user need; coverage shown in a traceability table.
- Wireframes / prototypes: cover the N priority journeys agreed in scope, at the agreed fidelity, in the agreed tool.
- Usability validation: moderated testing with at least N target users; task completion at or above X% on the priority tasks; a severity-rated issue log.
- Accessibility: design meets WCAG 2.1 AA on the audited screens; exceptions listed with rationale.

## Build and delivery

- Working capability: delivers the defined function on an agreed test set at or above X% success against the acceptance examples.
- Integration: connects to the named systems, with credentials handled per the client's policy, within the agreed guardrails.
- Handover: documentation and a runbook delivered; the client runs it unaided in a witnessed test.

## Training

- Sessions: N sessions delivered to up to M named participants; facilitator pack and participant materials handed over.
- Competence gain: a post-session measure shows a stated increase against the pre-session baseline on the target competencies.
- Application: participants produce a defined work product meeting the stated quality bar.

## Advisory and coaching

- Engagement: N sessions delivered to the named participant(s) over the agreed period; cadence met.
- Outcome (define carefully, advisory outcomes resist hard metrics): an agreed goal met against criteria set at the outset, captured by both parties. Prefer goal-attainment scaling to vanity satisfaction scores.
- Artefacts: action logs and a closing summary delivered.

## Process and ways of working

- Target state: defined and agreed; baseline captured; a stated improvement on the agreed flow metric (lead time, throughput, or similar) measured over the agreed window.
- Operating artefacts: process, board, or policy set delivered and in use; a first review cadence run with the client.

## General patterns that fit any service

- Documented and delivered: artefact exists in the agreed format, delivered to the agreed location, version-controlled.
- Reviewed and accepted: passes the acceptance process in the SOW within the review window.
- Threshold met: a named metric reaches a named target by a named method over a named window.
- Knowledge transferred: the client demonstrates the capability in a witnessed test, not just receives a document.

## Anti-patterns to reject

- "Improve", "optimise", "enhance", "support" with no measure or target.
- Satisfaction as the only measure ("client is happy"); subjective and unenforceable. If satisfaction is used, bind it to specific criteria agreed up front.
- Effort as the deliverable ("N days of work"); that is an input, not an outcome, and it reads inside-IR35.
- Targets you cannot control alone (e.g. "increase the client's win rate by X%") unless dependencies and attribution are pinned down; otherwise you carry risk you cannot manage. Flag these to the risk register.

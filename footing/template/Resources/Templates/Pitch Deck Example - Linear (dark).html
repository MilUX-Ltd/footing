Picket

Turn every soldier's phone into a passive drone-detection sensor. No new hardware. A section hears the threat before anyone sees it.

Team Picket/ European Defence Tech Hackathon/ London · Demo Day 2026
The gap

A one-way attack drone arrives
before any sensor is set up.

The first warning is a soldier hearing the motor. Today that signal dies in one person's ears. There is no cheap, deployable way to turn it into a shared, located alert across a section.

~2 minFrom "I can hear it" to impact. No time to deploy a fixed counter-UAS mast.
£0Spent on detection by a dismounted section that already carries phones and EUDs.
1 set of earsThe detection range of the current system: whoever happens to be listening.
Who feels it

We built this for the section,
not the headquarters.

Our user is a dismounted commander moving without overhead cover. They will not stop to configure a sensor, look at a laptop, or wait for a network. The tool has to work from the device already in their chest rig, in seconds, with gloves on.

END USER

Section 2IC, light role infantry

"I don't want another box to carry or charge. I want to know there's a drone, roughly where, and which way to move, before it's overhead. On the kit I already trust."

The idea

Every phone is already
a microphone and a clock.

Mesh a handful across a section. Each one listens for the acoustic signature of an FPV motor. Compare the arrival times and you get detection, classification, and a bearing, with zero new hardware.

How it works

Three steps. All on the edge.

01 · LISTEN

On-device model

A small acoustic classifier runs locally on each phone. No audio leaves the device; it only ever emits a timestamped detection event.

02 · SHARE

Low-bandwidth mesh

Detections gossip across the section over the existing radio or BLE mesh. Bytes, not streams. Works with no signal and no cloud.

03 · LOCATE

Time-difference of arrival

Four agreeing clocks turn four "I heard it" events into a bearing and a rough range, then a plain move-order on screen.

Why now

This was not buildable
three years ago.

  • THREATFPV and one-way attack drones are now the defining battlefield risk. Cheap, numerous, and acoustically distinctive.
  • DEVICERuggedised end-user devices are already issued. ATAK-class phones are in the section. We add software, not weight.
  • MODELOn-device ML is finally good and cheap enough to classify a motor in real time without draining the battery or phoning home.

Trained on ~600 open FPV audio clips plus our own recordings. Not production. Enough to show the physics holds.

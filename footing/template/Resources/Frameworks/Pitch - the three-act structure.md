# Pitch — the three-act structure

A founder's quick reference for building a short pitch, whether for a demo day, an investor meeting, or a customer. The idea underneath it: people think in narratives, not statistics, so a pitch is a story that starts a conversation, not a lecture that educates the room.

## The 3-minute pitch, on a clock

Three acts, about one minute each.

- **Act I, Set-up (0:00 to 1:00).** Intro and mission. The problem. Your solution in a line. Why now.
- **Act II, Confrontation (1:00 to 2:00).** Product, shown working. Value, quantified. Market. Competition, honestly.
- **Act III, Resolution (2:00 to 3:00).** Business model, team, traction. The ask. The one-line vision.

## The 60-second version

For corridors, a stand, or a first conversation. Five lines.

1. **Intro** — who are you?
2. **Hook** — why is this a problem or an opportunity?
3. **Solution** — what are you doing about it?
4. **Value** — how do you quantify the value to the customer?
5. **Call to action** — when do we meet again?

## The story spine

The narrative shape that makes a pitch stick. Use it on any beat.

- **Set-up:** When... Who... What... Where... then Context, Conflict, Solution, Complication.
- **Confrontation:** Because of that... And then... Until finally... Climax.
- **Resolution:** In the end... and one more thing (often the ask).

## Mental models: pitch like the left column

| Good | Bad |
|------|-----|
| A pitch starts a conversation | A pitch educates the audience |
| Slides are visual aids for my story | The slides remind me what to say |
| The deck clarifies my thinking | The deck helps me deal with investors |
| Fundraising is about trust | Fundraising is a necessary evil |
| Pitching is a skill | I'm not a natural presenter |
| Selling is persuasion | Selling is manipulation |
| It's about learning and process | Winning is all that matters |
| Feedback is growth | Feedback is overrated |
| It's uncertain and probabilistic | It's about control |

## Delivery

- **Pace: about 150 words a minute.** A three-minute pitch is roughly 450 words. Most people write twice that and run long without noticing. Cut to the clock.
- **The demo is the hero.** Slides support you; they are not the script.
- **Rehearse out loud, on a timer**, before anything else.

## Two tools worth using

- **Empathy-map the room before you pitch it.** Their role, their goals, what they are saying, their thoughts, their feelings and needs, their actions. Keep the end user in the room throughout.
- **Good process beats good outcome.** Early-stage work is a probabilistic game. A good process with a bad result (a demo that crashes) is better work than a win on luck. Judge the process.

## Five failure modes, and the question that fixes each

- **No user in the room.** You pitch the tech. Ask: who actually uses this, and what do they do in the moment?
- **Boiling the ocean.** Three products at once. Ask: if you could only ship one thing, which wins?
- **The demo is a diagram.** Slides where a screen should be. Ask: what can you show running, even if it is ugly?
- **No proof.** A roadmap pretending to be traction. Ask: what have you actually tested?
- **The soft ask.** "We'd love feedback." Ask: name the one pilot, partner, or sum that changes your next month.

# Frameworks Guide

## Purpose

`Frameworks/` holds sense-making and decision-making frameworks: Cynefin, Wardley Mapping, OKRs, Jobs To Be Done, others. Tools you reach for to think through specific kinds of problems.

Distinct from:

- `Resources/Methods/` — methodology theory (Kanban, Scrum, Lean).
- `Intelligence/sector-landscape/Frameworks/` — the procurement or commercial frameworks relevant to your sector, once you've added them. Different sense of "framework" entirely; those are commercial routes to market, not thinking tools.

## Add discipline

- One file per framework, or a subfolder if the framework has multiple pages.
- Capture: what the framework is, when to reach for it, a worked example or template if you've found one useful.
- Add a framework when you've used it at least once and expect to use it again. Reading-list candidates stay in `Books/` or `Reference/` until they've been applied.
